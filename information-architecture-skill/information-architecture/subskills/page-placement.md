# Page Placement Subskill

## Purpose

Decide where content belongs in a website structure so users can find it from navigation, search, related pages, and task pathways.

## Use this subskill when

- Creating a new page
- Moving a page
- Deciding whether content belongs under a department, service, topic, location, or audience section
- Resolving disagreements about page ownership versus page placement
- Recommending breadcrumbs or parent pages

## Placement criteria

Evaluate placement using these factors:

1. **Primary user intent**: what users are trying to do or understand.
2. **Content type**: service, form, guide, policy, news, event, contact, location, department page, dataset, or document.
3. **User vocabulary**: how users describe the need.
4. **Task relationship**: whether the page helps users apply, pay, request, register, report, appeal, contact, or check status.
5. **Topic relationship**: broader subject area or life event.
6. **Ownership**: department or team responsible for accuracy and updates.
7. **Governance**: review cadence, legal constraints, lifecycle, and archival requirements.

## Placement patterns

### Service pages

Place service pages under the topic or task category where users expect to start. Department ownership can be shown on the page but should not be the only path.

### Department pages

Use department pages for organizational information, leadership, responsibilities, public meetings, and contact channels. Do not bury high-demand resident tasks only inside department pages.

### Forms and documents

Forms and documents should usually be attached to the service or process they support, not isolated in a file library without context.

### News and events

News and events can support topical findability through tags and related links, but they should not replace evergreen service or policy pages.

### Location-based content

Use location pages when the user intent depends on place, facility, office, counter service, hours, directions, or jurisdiction.

## Output format

Provide:

- Recommended parent page or section
- Recommended breadcrumb path
- Alternative placements considered
- Rationale based on user intent
- Required cross-links from other likely entry points
- Ownership and maintenance notes
- Redirect or migration notes if moving an existing page

## Review checklist

- Does the page live where users would look first?
- Is there a clear parent-child relationship?
- Are ownership and user-facing placement separated where needed?
- Are secondary entry points covered by links or taxonomy?
- Would search users understand the page from title and snippet?
