# Forms and Transactions Subskill

## Purpose

Improve forms, applications, payments, uploads, signatures, registrations, reports, and other resident transactions so they are easier to start, complete, submit, and recover from.

## When to use

Use when reviewing or drafting form instructions, online forms, PDFs, payment flows, intake questions, upload requirements, signature blocks, application packets, or transactional confirmation messages.

## Core rules

- Explain who should use the form before asking for information.
- State estimated completion time, required documents, fees, and deadlines before the form starts.
- Ask only for information needed to complete the service.
- Group related fields together.
- Label fields using terms residents understand.
- Explain why sensitive or unusual information is needed.
- Mark required fields clearly.
- Put help text where the resident needs it.
- Use examples for confusing fields.
- Preserve the resident’s work when errors occur whenever possible.
- Provide a confirmation, receipt, case number, or next-step message after submission.

## Transaction checklist

Before the resident starts, state:

- Who can use this form or transaction
- What the form does
- What the form does not do
- What information is required
- What documents are required
- Accepted file types and size limits, if uploads are required
- Fees and accepted payment methods
- Deadline or service window
- Estimated completion time
- Whether the resident can save and return
- What confirmation the resident will receive

## Form question review

For each field or question, check:

- Is this information required for the service outcome?
- Does the resident know the answer?
- Is the field label clear without internal jargon?
- Is the expected format obvious?
- Is help text needed?
- Is the field placed near related fields?
- Can this information be prefilled or avoided?
- What happens if the resident leaves it blank?

## Payment flows

For payments, state:

- Amount or method for calculating amount
- Due date
- Accepted payment methods
- Processing fees, if any
- Refund policy or non-refundable status, if relevant
- What happens after payment
- What proof of payment the resident receives
- What to do if payment fails or is charged incorrectly

## Uploads and attachments

For uploads, state:

- Exact document names or acceptable alternatives
- File types accepted
- File size limits
- Whether photos are accepted
- Whether originals, scans, or copies are required
- What happens if documents are missing or unreadable

## Quality criteria

A form or transaction is ready when residents understand whether to use it, what they need, how long it will take, how to complete it, what proof they receive, and how to recover if something goes wrong.
