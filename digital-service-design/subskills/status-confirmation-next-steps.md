# Status, Confirmation, and Next Steps Subskill

## Purpose

Design confirmation messages, receipts, status updates, and next-step instructions that tell residents what happened, what happens next, when to expect action, and how to get help.

## When to use

Use for submission screens, confirmation emails, payment receipts, appointment confirmations, status pages, case updates, notices, application follow-ups, and staff response templates.

## Core rules

- Confirm the resident’s completed action in plain language.
- Provide a confirmation number, case number, receipt number, transaction ID, or other proof when available.
- State what the County will do next.
- State what the resident must do next, if anything.
- Give realistic timing or timing ranges.
- Explain how to check status.
- Include contact or help options.
- Warn about deadlines, consequences, missing information, or no-response periods when relevant.

## Confirmation message structure

Use this pattern:

1. **Result** — “We received your [application/request/report/payment].”
2. **Proof** — “Your confirmation number is [number].”
3. **What happens next** — “We will [review/contact/process/inspect] within [timeframe].”
4. **What you need to do** — “You need to [action],” or “You do not need to do anything else now.”
5. **Status** — “You can check status by [method].”
6. **Help** — “Contact [office/channel] if [condition].”

## Status message types

### Received
Use when the County has received the submission but has not started or completed review.

### In review
Use when staff or a system is evaluating the request, documents, payment, eligibility, or availability.

### More information needed
Use when the resident must provide missing or corrected information.

### Approved or accepted
Use when the service can proceed or the resident has qualified.

### Denied or not eligible
Use when the service cannot proceed and the resident needs reasons, alternatives, or appeal rights.

### Completed or closed
Use when the County has fulfilled, closed, or resolved the request.

## Status-message template

## Status: [Status name]

[Plain-language summary of current status.]

## What this means
[Explain the consequence or current stage.]

## What happens next
[County action and timing.]

## What you need to do
[Resident action, if any.]

## If you have questions
[Contact, status-check method, or support path.]

## Review questions

- Does the resident know whether their action was successful?
- Does the resident have proof they can save?
- Does the message explain timing?
- Does it distinguish County action from resident action?
- Does it explain how to check status?
- Does it tell the resident what to do if nothing happens?
- Does it include help for mistakes, missing information, or changed circumstances?

## Quality criteria

A confirmation or status message is ready when the resident knows what happened, has proof, knows what happens next, knows whether they must act, and knows how to get help.
