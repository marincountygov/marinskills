# Eligibility and Requirements Subskill

## Purpose

Clarify who can use a service, who cannot use it, what is required before starting, and what conditions affect the result.

## When to use

Use when drafting or reviewing service pages, program pages, benefit pages, permit pages, application instructions, registration pages, appeals, renewals, and intake forms.

## Core rules

- Put eligibility and requirements before the start action.
- Distinguish eligibility from required documents and process steps.
- State disqualifying conditions clearly.
- Explain conditional requirements using “if” statements.
- Avoid legal or administrative phrasing unless legally required.
- Provide alternatives when the resident is not eligible or lacks a requirement.
- State whether staff will verify eligibility after submission.

## Definitions

- **Eligibility**: who may use the service or qualify for the outcome.
- **Requirements**: what the resident must provide, do, pay, prove, sign, or complete.
- **Conditions**: facts that change the resident’s path, such as location, income, age, property type, deadline, prior approval, or household status.
- **Disqualifiers**: facts that mean the resident should not start this service or should use a different service.

## Who should not use this service
- [Disqualifying condition and alternative path]

## What you need before you start
- [Document, information, account, authorization, payment method, or signature]
- [Document, information, account, authorization, payment method, or signature]

## If your situation is different
- If [condition], [action or alternative path].
- If [condition], [action or alternative path].

## How eligibility is reviewed
[Explain whether the resident self-checks eligibility, staff verifies it, or both.]

## Review questions

- Can the resident decide whether this service is for them?
- Are eligibility rules separate from required documents?
- Are disqualifiers clear?
- Are alternatives provided for people who are not eligible?
- Are conditional paths written as clear “if” statements?
- Are deadlines, location limits, income limits, age limits, property limits, or account requirements visible?
- Does the page explain what happens if the resident applies but is not eligible?

Eligibility and requirements content is ready when residents can decide whether to start, what to prepare, what could prevent approval, and where to go if this is not the right service.
