# Synonyms and Plain-Language Keywords

## Purpose
Connect official terminology with the words residents use so content can be found through search without sacrificing accuracy.

## Use when
- Official names are technical, legal, or program-specific.
- Residents use several names for the same service.
- Internal search results miss relevant pages.
- A page needs better keyword coverage without keyword stuffing.

## Principles

1. Pair official terms with plain-language terms.
   - Example: `transient occupancy tax` and `hotel tax`.
   - Example: `recorder` and `birth certificate`.

2. Explain acronyms on first use.
   - Use acronym-only optimization only when the acronym is widely searched and understood.

3. Use synonyms where they help comprehension.
   - Synonyms can appear in headings, body text, metadata, search summaries, and internal search configuration.
   - Do not force every synonym into visible copy.

4. Preserve legal or policy precision.
   - Do not replace a required legal term if doing so changes meaning.
   - Add plain-language explanation beside the official term.

5. Keep synonym sets controlled.
   - Avoid conflicting terms for the same thing across pages.
   - Use one preferred label when possible.

## Synonym mapping pattern

For each concept:

- Preferred public term
- Official term
- Common resident terms
- Acronyms
- Misspellings or variants, if useful
- Terms not to use
- Recommended page or canonical destination

## Examples

- Preferred public term: `food benefits`
  - Official term: `CalFresh`
  - Related terms: `food stamps`, `EBT`, `nutrition assistance`

- Preferred public term: `property tax bill`
  - Official term: `secured property tax bill`
  - Related terms: `tax bill`, `pay property tax`, `parcel tax bill`

## Review checklist

- Does each synonym point to the right page?
- Are outdated or stigmatizing terms avoided in visible content unless needed for search matching?
- Are official terms still available for users who know them?
- Are synonyms consistent across related pages?
