# Resident Search Terms

## Purpose
Identify and apply search terms residents are likely to use when looking for County services, information, forms, or help.

## Use when
- Planning a new page.
- Rewriting a service page.
- Reviewing search performance.
- Creating synonyms for internal search.
- Mapping old content to new pages.

## Search intent categories

1. Task intent
   - apply for
   - pay
   - report
   - request
   - renew
   - register
   - appeal
   - check eligibility
   - make an appointment

2. Problem intent
   - noise complaint
   - pothole
   - illegal dumping
   - lost dog
   - eviction help
   - food assistance

3. Document intent
   - form
   - application
   - permit
   - certificate
   - notice
   - agenda
   - records

4. Status intent
   - application status
   - permit status
   - inspection results
   - meeting date
   - payment confirmation

5. Location intent
   - near me
   - in Marin County
   - unincorporated Marin
   - specific city or community name when relevant

## Process

1. Name the official service, program, or document.
2. Translate the official term into likely resident words.
3. Identify action verbs tied to the task.
4. Include common variants, misspellings, and acronyms only when useful.
5. Decide where terms belong:
   - title,
   - H1,
   - headings,
   - body copy,
   - metadata,
   - internal search synonym list.

## Output format

For each page, provide:

- Primary resident term
- Secondary resident terms
- Official term
- Common synonyms
- Terms to avoid
- Recommended placement
- Content gap, if users search for something the page does not answer

## Review checklist

- Are the terms based on what residents are trying to do?
- Are official names connected to plain-language equivalents?
- Are acronyms explained?
- Are high-intent terms placed naturally in headings or body content?
- Are irrelevant popular terms excluded?
