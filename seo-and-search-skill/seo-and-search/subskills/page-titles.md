# Page Titles

## Purpose
Create page titles and H1s that improve search result clarity, resident comprehension, and task completion.

## Use when
- Naming a new page.
- Reviewing title tags or H1s.
- Migrating pages from legacy content.
- Improving pages that perform poorly in search.
- Resolving vague, duplicate, or department-centered titles.

## Principles

1. Lead with the resident task or subject.
   - Use: `Apply for a building permit`
   - Avoid: `Permit Center Services Division`

2. Be specific enough to distinguish the page.
   - Use: `Pay property taxes`
   - Avoid: `Payments`

3. Keep title tags concise.
   - Put the strongest distinguishing words first.
   - Avoid long department chains unless needed for clarity.

4. Match the title, H1, and page purpose.
   - The title tag can be slightly more search-oriented.
   - The H1 should be clear and natural on the page.
   - Do not use a title that promises content the page does not provide.

5. Use official names only when they help users.
   - Include program names after the plain-language task when needed.
   - Example: `Apply for food benefits through CalFresh`.

## Recommended pattern

For service pages:

`[Action] [service/object]`

Examples:
- `Apply for a marriage license`
- `Report illegal dumping`
- `Request public records`
- `Register to vote`
- `Appeal a property assessment`

For informational pages:

`[Topic] in Marin County`

Examples:
- `Short-term rental rules in Marin County`
- `Wildfire preparedness in Marin County`

For official notices:

`[Notice type]: [specific subject]`

Examples:
- `Public notice: Road closure on Sir Francis Drake Boulevard`
- `Notice of public hearing: Housing element amendment`

## Review checklist

- Does the title answer “What is this page about?”
- Does it use resident language?
- Is the title distinct from similar pages?
- Does the H1 reinforce the same purpose?
- Does it avoid acronyms unless they are widely recognized?
- Does it avoid internal office names as the primary label?
