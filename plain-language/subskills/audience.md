# Audience Sub-skill

Use this sub-skill when the content problem is reader fit: unclear audience, wrong assumptions, expert-centered wording, or missing user questions.

## Main rule

Write for the actual audience, not for reviewers, attorneys, managers, or internal specialists unless they are the intended readers.

## Steps

1. Identify the reader group.
2. Identify what the reader is trying to do.
3. Identify what the reader already knows.
4. Identify what the reader needs to know next.
5. Identify the best outcome for the organization and for the reader.
6. Rewrite around the reader's task.

## Review questions

- Who needs this information?
- Why are they reading it?
- What action do they need to take?
- What decision do they need to make?
- What question are they most likely to ask first?
- What terms will they not know?
- What details can be moved later, linked, or removed?

## Rewrite tactics

- Address the reader as "you" when appropriate.
- Explain why the information matters to the reader.
- Replace internal program structure with reader task structure.
- Segment content for different audiences when one page tries to serve several groups.
- Do not oversimplify for expert audiences; plain language means fitting the audience, not writing for the lowest possible reading level.
