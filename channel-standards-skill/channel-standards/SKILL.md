# Channel Standards Skill

## Purpose
Adapt messages appropriately across web, email, social, SMS, flyers, agenda packets, press releases, video, and public-meeting materials.

Use this skill when a user asks to create, review, rewrite, adapt, or compare public-facing content across one or more communication channels.

## Core principle
Do not simply copy the same message into every channel. Preserve the policy meaning and facts, but adapt structure, emphasis, length, call to action, and formatting to the channel, audience, urgency, and user task.

## What this skill does
- Identifies the primary channel and secondary channels.
- Clarifies the resident action or understanding the content must support.
- Converts source information into channel-appropriate formats.
- Flags missing facts required for a channel, such as dates, location, contact information, eligibility, deadlines, links, or meeting access details.
- Keeps messages consistent across channels while allowing the shape and level of detail to vary.
- Routes specialized work to the correct subskill.

## Subskill routing
Use the most relevant subskill:

- `web-pages.md` for service pages, landing pages, news items, alerts, and web content.
- `email-newsletters.md` for email campaigns, newsletters, listserv messages, and stakeholder updates.
- `social-media.md` for social posts, captions, threads, carousel copy, and platform-specific summaries.
- `sms-alerts.md` for short text alerts, urgent notices, reminders, and opt-in messaging.
- `press-releases.md` for formal media announcements and public information releases.
- `public-meeting-content.md` for agenda items, staff-report summaries, meeting notices, speaker instructions, and hearing materials.
- `video-audio-scripts.md` for scripts, narration, interview prompts, public-service announcements, and caption-oriented copy.
- `print-to-digital-conversion.md` for converting flyers, PDFs, mailers, brochures, and static handouts into web-first or digital-first content.

## Required intake questions
When the user has not provided enough information, infer what you can and mark assumptions. Ask only for information that materially affects accuracy, legality, timing, or publication readiness.

For any channel, determine:
- Audience: residents, applicants, permittees, voters, clients, businesses, vendors, media, community partners, internal staff, or elected bodies.
- Purpose: inform, persuade, invite, remind, warn, collect input, explain a decision, request action, or provide status.
- User task: apply, pay, report, request, register, comment, attend, appeal, contact, prepare, or check eligibility.
- Required facts: who, what, when, where, why, how, cost, deadline, eligibility, contact, language access, and link.
- Sensitivity: emergency, legal, privacy, procurement, enforcement, public health, public safety, or controversy.
- Lifecycle: draft, review, publish, update, expire, archive, or replace.

## Output expectations
When producing content, include:
- Recommended channel or channel mix.
- Channel-specific version of the message.
- Required metadata or publishing notes when relevant.
- Missing information or assumptions.
- Review or escalation flags.

When reviewing content, return:
- What works.
- What creates channel mismatch.
- Specific edits by channel.
- Risks or missing facts.
- A publication-ready rewrite when feasible.

## Channel adaptation rules
- Put the user action first when the channel supports action.
- Put the most time-sensitive fact near the beginning.
- Keep web content durable and scannable.
- Keep email subject lines specific and non-clickbait.
- Keep social posts concise, accessible, and link-oriented.
- Keep SMS alerts short, direct, and reserved for high-value or time-sensitive messages.
- Keep press releases factual, attributed, and media-ready.
- Keep public-meeting content procedurally clear and neutral.
- Keep scripts spoken, paced, and caption-friendly.
- Convert print-first content into structured digital content rather than uploading a static document as the default.

## Boundaries
This skill does not replace plain-language, accessibility, legal, privacy, emergency-management, records-retention, or brand-review skills. If those issues are material, identify the need and route or escalate accordingly.

## Quality checklist
Before finalizing, verify:
- The channel matches the user need.
- The message has one primary action or takeaway.
- Dates, times, locations, links, and contacts are present or flagged as missing.
- The tone is appropriate for public-sector communication.
- The content can be understood without internal context.
- The same facts are consistent across all requested channels.
- The content has an obvious next step.
