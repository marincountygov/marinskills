# Public Meeting Content Subskill

## Use when
Creating or reviewing agenda descriptions, staff-report summaries, public hearing notices, meeting announcements, comment instructions, speaker cards, presentations, and agenda-packet summaries.

## Goal
Help residents understand what will be considered, why it matters, how to participate, and what decision or action may occur.

## Public-meeting standards
- State the meeting body, date, time, location, and participation options.
- Identify the item, proposed action, and decision point.
- Explain public impact without advocacy unless the role requires a recommendation.
- Provide clear public-comment instructions.
- Distinguish informational items from action items.
- Avoid jargon such as resolution numbers or staff report references without plain context.
- Include deadlines for written comments when applicable.
- Route legal notice requirements for proper review.

## Recommended structure
1. Meeting body and date.
2. Agenda item or topic.
3. What is being considered.
4. Why it matters.
5. Possible action.
6. How to attend or watch.
7. How to comment.
8. Where to read supporting materials.
9. Contact for questions.

## Review checklist
- Can a resident tell whether this affects them?
- Is the decision point clear?
- Are meeting access and comment instructions complete?
- Is the language neutral and procedurally accurate?
- Are deadlines and supporting links included?
- Are legal-notice or agenda-posting requirements flagged?

## Output format
Provide meeting-ready copy, public summary, comment instructions, web or agenda-packet version, and missing facts.
