# Web Pages Subskill

## Use when
Creating or reviewing service pages, department pages, landing pages, news posts, alerts, event pages, forms pages, and other web content.

## Goal
Make web content findable, scannable, task-oriented, and durable.

## Web-page standards
- Start with the resident need, not the department structure.
- Use a descriptive H1 that matches the task or topic.
- Put the primary action near the top.
- Use short sections with descriptive headings.
- Prefer structured fields for dates, locations, fees, deadlines, eligibility, and contact information.
- Link to the canonical source rather than duplicating large blocks of content.
- Avoid PDF-only publication when the information is needed as web content.
- Separate durable service information from time-bound announcements.

## Recommended structure
1. Page title.
2. One-sentence purpose or summary.
3. Primary action button or link.
4. Who can use this service or information.
5. What you need before you start.
6. Steps or process.
7. Cost, deadline, and processing time.
8. Contact or help.
9. Related pages.

## Review checklist
- Does the page answer what the user came to do?
- Is the title specific enough to search for?
- Is the primary call to action visible without reading the entire page?
- Are eligibility, fees, deadlines, and processing times clear?
- Are links descriptive and current?
- Is outdated or duplicate content removed?

## Output format
Provide a web-ready outline or rewrite with headings, page summary, calls to action, links needed, metadata suggestions, and missing facts.
