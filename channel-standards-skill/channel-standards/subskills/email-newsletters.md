# Email Newsletters Subskill

## Use when
Creating or reviewing newsletters, listserv messages, stakeholder emails, subscription updates, campaign emails, and digest-style communications.

## Goal
Make email content specific, skimmable, actionable, and appropriate for subscribers who may read quickly or on mobile devices.

## Email standards
- Use a specific subject line that states the topic and value.
- Put the most important update or action first.
- Keep the opening short.
- Use sections for multiple items.
- Make each item answer: what changed, why it matters, and what to do next.
- Use one primary call to action per item.
- Avoid relying on images to carry essential information.
- Include required contact, deadline, or link information.

## Newsletter structure
1. Subject line.
2. Preview text.
3. Short opening summary.
4. Primary item or call to action.
5. Secondary items grouped by topic.
6. Dates and deadlines.
7. Contact or help.
8. Footer notes, if required.

## Review checklist
- Is the subject line accurate and non-clickbait?
- Can the reader understand the message from the first screen?
- Are deadlines and calls to action obvious?
- Are links labeled by destination or action?
- Is the email too long for the channel?
- Are multiple audiences being mixed without clear segmentation?

## Output format
Provide subject line options, preview text, email body, call-to-action labels, segmentation notes, and missing facts.
