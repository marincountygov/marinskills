# Video and Audio Scripts Subskill

## Use when
Creating or reviewing video scripts, audio scripts, narration, public-service announcements, interview prompts, explainer videos, reels, voiceover text, and caption-oriented scripts.

## Goal
Make spoken public information clear, paced, accurate, and usable with captions or transcripts.

## Script standards
- Write for speech, not for a printed memo.
- Use short sentences and natural transitions.
- Put the main point near the start.
- Avoid long lists unless they are visually supported.
- Spell out acronyms on first use or avoid them.
- Include pronunciation notes for names, places, and technical terms when needed.
- Make essential visual information available in spoken or caption text.
- End with a clear next step.

## Recommended structure
1. Opening hook or public need.
2. Context in plain language.
3. Key facts or steps.
4. What the resident should do.
5. Link, phone number, or meeting detail.
6. Closing line.

## Review checklist
- Does the script sound natural when read aloud?
- Is the pace reasonable?
- Are names, dates, and contact details clear?
- Is the call to action repeated or shown clearly?
- Can the content work with captions and transcript?
- Is sensitive or technical language explained?

## Output format
Provide script, runtime estimate, caption-friendly version or notes, on-screen text suggestions, pronunciation notes, and missing facts.
