# Print-to-Digital Conversion Subskill

## Use when
Converting flyers, PDFs, brochures, mailers, posters, handouts, agenda packet excerpts, or printed notices into web, email, social, SMS, or other digital formats.

## Goal
Transform print-first content into structured digital content rather than simply reproducing the print artifact online.

## Conversion standards
- Identify the original print purpose and the digital user task.
- Extract essential facts before rewriting.
- Convert visual hierarchy into headings, summaries, lists, and calls to action.
- Replace dense blocks with structured sections.
- Turn printed URLs or QR-code destinations into descriptive links.
- Preserve required legal or notice language when needed, but separate it from user-task content where appropriate.
- Do not assume a PDF is the best digital format.
- Recommend expiration, archive, or replacement logic for time-bound print material.

## Conversion process
1. Identify audience and purpose.
2. Extract facts: who, what, when, where, why, how, cost, deadline, contact, link.
3. Identify the primary digital channel.
4. Create a web-first or channel-first version.
5. Flag missing or outdated information.
6. Recommend whether the source print/PDF should remain linked.

## Review checklist
- Is the converted version usable on a phone?
- Is the key action available without opening a PDF?
- Are links descriptive?
- Are dates and deadlines current?
- Are images or layout-dependent details converted into text?
- Is duplicate or obsolete content controlled?

## Output format
Provide extracted facts, recommended channel, converted copy, link and metadata notes, and source-document disposition recommendation.
