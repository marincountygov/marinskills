# Press Releases Subskill

## Use when
Creating or reviewing press releases, media advisories, formal announcements, and public information releases.

## Goal
Produce factual, media-ready announcements with clear news value, attribution, context, and contact information.

## Press-release standards
- Lead with the news, decision, event, or public impact.
- Include dateline, date, and jurisdiction when appropriate.
- Attribute opinions, policy rationale, or quotes.
- Keep factual claims verifiable.
- Include who is affected, what changed, when it happens, and what the public should do.
- Separate background from the main announcement.
- Include media contact information.
- Avoid promotional language unless it is clearly part of an attributed quote.

## Recommended structure
1. Headline.
2. Subhead or summary.
3. Dateline and lead paragraph.
4. Details and public impact.
5. Quote, if provided or requested.
6. Background.
7. Public action or link.
8. Media contact.
9. Boilerplate, if needed.

## Review checklist
- Is there a clear news hook?
- Are facts, dates, titles, and names verified or flagged?
- Are quotes attributed correctly?
- Is the public action clear?
- Is media contact information included?
- Does the release avoid unsupported claims?

## Output format
Provide a press-release draft, headline options, quote placeholders if quotes are not supplied, media-contact placeholder, and verification notes.
