# Prepublication Checklist Subskill

## Purpose
Run a final publication checklist before a page, document, form, message, campaign, service flow, or public notice goes live.

## Use this when

- The user asks whether something is ready to publish.
- A page or document is going through final review.
- A message will be sent through email, SMS, social, press, print, or web.
- A form, application, registration, payment, request, report, or appeal flow is about to launch.

## Checklist

### Purpose and audience
- The page or message has one clear primary purpose.
- The intended audience is explicit or inferable.
- The content answers the user's likely first questions.
- The content avoids internal-only assumptions, jargon, and unexplained program names.

### Task completion
- The next action is clear.
- Eligibility, requirements, cost, deadline, processing time, and contact options are present when relevant.
- Forms, applications, payment links, registration links, or request links are easy to find.
- Confirmation and next steps are explained for transactions.

### Structure and readability
- The title is specific and resident-facing.
- Headings describe the content below them.
- The most important information appears first.
- Lists, tables, or steps are used where they improve scanning.
- Sentences and paragraphs are short enough for fast comprehension.

### Governance and risk
- Owner, reviewer, approval path, and review date are known.
- Legal, policy, privacy, procurement, emergency, or retention issues are escalated where needed.
- Contact information is current.
- Dates, deadlines, locations, fees, phone numbers, links, and names are verified.

### Channel and publication mechanics
- The content fits the channel.
- Page placement, navigation label, metadata, and cross-links support findability.
- Downloadable documents are necessary and named clearly.
- Links work and point to canonical destinations.
- Images, charts, tables, and embeds have appropriate alternatives or explanatory text.

## Output

Return:

- Decision: ready, ready with minor edits, hold, or escalate.
- Required fixes before publication.
- Recommended improvements.
- Items requiring owner confirmation.
- Retest steps after fixes.
