# Publication Readiness Subskill

## Purpose
Produce a clear publication recommendation based on QA findings, unresolved risks, and required approvals.

## Use this when

- The user asks for a final readiness decision.
- QA findings need to be converted into a launch recommendation.
- A content owner needs to know whether to publish, hold, or escalate.

## Readiness decisions

### Ready
Use when there are no blockers or high-risk unresolved issues, required approvals are complete, and only low-risk improvements remain.

### Ready with minor edits
Use when only low or manageable medium issues remain and they can be fixed quickly without changing meaning, policy, eligibility, legal obligations, or task flow.

### Hold
Use when unresolved issues are likely to harm task completion, create confusion, publish incorrect information, expose risk, or undermine trust.

### Escalate
Use when a decision is needed from legal, privacy, public records, procurement, emergency management, executive leadership, accessibility specialists, translation reviewers, or the content owner.

## Decision factors

- Accuracy of facts, dates, fees, deadlines, contacts, and requirements.
- Resident task completion and next-step clarity.
- Ownership and approval status.
- Legal, policy, privacy, procurement, records, and emergency risks.
- Accessibility and multilingual review needs.
- Channel fit, IA, metadata, and search readiness.
- Brand and editorial quality.
- Retest evidence.

## Output

Use this structure:

**Recommendation:** Ready / Ready with minor edits / Hold / Escalate

**Reason:** One concise paragraph.

**Must fix before publication:**
- Item, owner, and retest step.

**Can fix after publication:**
- Item, owner, and target timing.

**Approvals or escalation needed:**
- Role or team and reason.

**Retest checklist:**
- Concrete verification steps.
