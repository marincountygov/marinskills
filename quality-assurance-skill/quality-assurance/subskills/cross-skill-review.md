# Cross-Skill Review Subskill

## Purpose
Coordinate a broad QA review across related content standards without duplicating every specialist skill.

## Use this when

- The asset crosses multiple review domains.
- The user asks for a comprehensive review.
- The content includes public-facing copy, design, metadata, forms, links, governance, and service instructions.
- Several skills may need to be applied in sequence.

## Review domains

### Brand and visual standards
Check whether the asset appears consistent with approved County visual identity, logo use, color use, layout conventions, and tone appropriate to the channel.

### Accessibility
Route to the separate accessibility skill or reviewer when the asset includes web content, PDFs, forms, images, charts, tables, video, audio, documents, or interactive components. Do not duplicate detailed accessibility testing here.

### Plain language
Check for user-centered language, active voice, short sentences, clear headings, plain words, and action-oriented structure.

### Content strategy
Check why the content exists, who it serves, where it belongs, how long it should live, and how success will be measured.

### Information architecture
Check navigation labels, page placement, search snippets, cross-links, canonical pages, and topic taxonomy.

### Channel standards
Check whether the message is adapted to the delivery channel rather than copied unchanged across channels.

### Governance
Check ownership, review cycle, approval routing, privacy, legal/policy risk, public records, procurement/vendor concerns, and emergency notice escalation.

### Inclusive and multilingual language
Check for respectful, resident-centered language and route multilingual or language-access needs to specialist review.

### Forms, documents, and service design
Check whether forms, notices, letters, PDFs, downloads, and transaction flows support successful resident task completion.

### Data visualization
Check whether charts, maps, dashboards, tables, and data notes are readable, interpretable, and supported by appropriate caveats.

## Output

Use a matrix:

| Domain | Status | Finding | Severity | Recommended action | Owner/escalation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Statuses: pass, needs edit, needs specialist review, not applicable, insufficient evidence.
