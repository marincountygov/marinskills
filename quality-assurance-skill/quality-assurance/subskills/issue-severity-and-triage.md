# Issue Severity and Triage Subskill

## Purpose
Classify QA findings by impact, urgency, and escalation path so teams can decide what must be fixed before publication.

## Use this when

- A review found multiple issues.
- The user asks what matters most.
- Publication timing requires prioritization.
- Issues need assignment to different owners.

## Severity levels

### Blocker
Prevents publication or requires immediate takedown/escalation.

Common examples:
- Wrong legal, safety, emergency, eligibility, deadline, fee, or application information.
- Privacy exposure or sensitive personal information.
- Broken primary transaction or service path.
- Missing required approval for high-risk content.
- Public notice defect that may affect rights, deadlines, or compliance.

### High
Should be fixed before publication unless an emergency publishing decision is made.

Common examples:
- Unclear next steps for a high-volume or high-impact service.
- Misleading title, metadata, navigation, or links.
- Significant plain-language or translation-readiness problem.
- Form instructions likely to cause failed submissions.
- Duplicate or obsolete content likely to confuse residents.

### Medium
Should be fixed before or soon after publication depending on timing and audience impact.

Common examples:
- Weak headings, inconsistent labels, incomplete context, or minor task friction.
- Metadata gaps that reduce search quality.
- Noncritical broken secondary links.
- Formatting problems that reduce scanning.

### Low
Polish item or minor consistency issue.

Common examples:
- Style inconsistencies.
- Minor word choice improvements.
- Nonblocking layout refinements.

## Triage fields

For each issue, capture:

- Severity.
- Affected audience or task.
- Risk type.
- Evidence.
- Recommended action.
- Owner.
- Deadline.
- Retest method.
- Escalation needed.

## Output

Use a table sorted by severity, then provide a short recommended triage sequence.
