# Age and Income Sensitive Language Subskill

## Use when
Content refers to older adults, youth, children, minors, families, income, poverty, homelessness, benefits, fees, debt, fines, affordability, financial hardship, or economic status.

## Goals
- Avoid patronizing, shaming, or deficit-focused language.
- Use accurate terms for age groups, eligibility, income, and housing status.
- Preserve clear instructions about services, costs, deadlines, documentation, and options.

## Guidance

1. Avoid patronizing age language.
   - Prefer `older adults` to `the elderly` unless a program uses a specific statutory term.
   - Refer to children, youth, teens, young adults, or minors based on the actual age range and legal context.

2. Do not define people by poverty or benefits.
   - Prefer `people with low income`, `households with limited income`, or the specific program term.
   - Avoid `the poor`, `welfare cases`, `needy families`, `indigent people`, or `burden` framing unless quoting a required legal term.

3. Use respectful housing-status language.
   - Prefer `people experiencing homelessness`, `people without stable housing`, or the specific housing situation.
   - Avoid `the homeless` as a noun, `vagrants`, `transients`, or blame-focused phrasing.

4. Be clear about money.
   - State fees, penalties, payment options, waivers, exemptions, due dates, and appeal rights plainly.
   - Avoid euphemisms that hide cost or consequences.

5. Avoid shame in compliance content.
   - Instead of implying irresponsibility, state the requirement, reason, deadline, available help, and consequence.

## Preferred rewrites

- `older adults` instead of `the elderly`.
- `people experiencing homelessness` instead of `the homeless`.
- `households with low income` instead of `poor families`.
- `people who receive benefits` instead of `welfare recipients` when the program name is not needed.
- `You may qualify for a fee waiver` instead of `If you cannot afford this` when the waiver is the key action.

## Escalate when
- The content concerns benefits eligibility, means testing, child welfare, elder abuse, homelessness response, debt collection, fines, evictions, liens, public assistance, or legal notices.
