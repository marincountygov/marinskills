# Gender, Family, and Identity Language Subskill

## Use when
Content refers to gender, pronouns, sexual orientation, marital status, pregnancy, parenting, family structure, domestic partnerships, caregivers, households, or relationship status.

## Goals
- Avoid assumptions about gender, relationships, family composition, caregiving, or household roles.
- Use inclusive language while preserving legal, program, and eligibility clarity.
- Respect names, pronouns, and self-identification.

## Guidance

1. Use gender-neutral terms when gender is not relevant.
   - Prefer `they`, `person`, `resident`, `applicant`, `parent or guardian`, `spouse or partner`, `chair`, `staff member`, or role-specific terms.
   - Avoid defaulting to `he/she`, `husband/wife`, `mother/father`, or gendered occupational terms unless needed.

2. Preserve chosen names and pronouns.
   - Use the name and pronouns provided by the person or source.
   - If pronouns are unknown, rewrite to avoid pronouns or use the person’s name.

3. Avoid assumptions about families and households.
   - Use `parent or guardian`, `caregiver`, `household member`, `family member`, or `support person` where appropriate.
   - Do not assume two-parent households, marital status, biological relationships, or gender roles.

4. Keep eligibility and legal terms clear.
   - If a program uses legal categories such as `spouse`, `dependent`, `legal guardian`, `domestic partner`, `custodial parent`, or `minor`, retain the required term and explain what it means in the program context.

5. Use care with pregnancy and reproductive health references.
   - Match the language to the policy, clinical, or program context.
   - Consider `pregnant people` when the service is not limited by gender identity, unless a legal or clinical source requires a different formulation.

## Preferred rewrites

- `parent or guardian` instead of `mother and father` when both are not specifically required.
- `spouse or partner` instead of `husband or wife` when marital gender is irrelevant.
- `staffed by police officers` instead of `manned by policemen`.
- `people who are pregnant` instead of `expectant mothers` when inclusive and accurate for the service.
- `household members` instead of `family members` when eligibility applies by residence rather than relationship.

## Escalate when
- The content concerns protected-class rights, harassment, discrimination, domestic violence, child custody, reproductive health, employment benefits, school records, legal parentage, or official identity documents.
