# Race, Ethnicity, and National Origin Language Subskill

## Use when
Content refers to race, ethnicity, national origin, immigration, tribal affiliation, language access, cultural groups, demographic data, equity analysis, or community outreach.

## Goals
- Use accurate, respectful, and community-centered terminology.
- Avoid stereotypes, assumptions, and unnecessary identity references.
- Preserve clarity for services, eligibility, data, outreach, and civil rights obligations.

## Guidance

1. Include identity only when relevant.
   - Use racial, ethnic, national origin, or immigration descriptors only when they are needed for context, services, data, eligibility, outreach, civil rights, or public reporting.

2. Use the terms people use for themselves.
   - Preserve community self-identification where known.
   - Avoid treating broad categories as interchangeable. For example, do not use `Hispanic`, `Latino`, `Latina`, `Latinx`, `Chicano`, or a country-of-origin term as equivalents unless the source or community context supports it.

3. Be precise with geography and status.
   - Distinguish race, ethnicity, nationality, language, citizenship, immigration status, and country of origin.
   - Avoid using `foreign`, `alien`, or `illegal` as a noun for people unless quoting or explaining a legal term that must be used.

4. Use neutral language for immigration and language access.
   - Prefer `immigrants`, `people applying for asylum`, `people without current immigration status`, or the specific legally required term.
   - Prefer `people who speak [language]` or `people who prefer [language]` over `non-English speakers` when possible.

5. Handle tribal and Indigenous references carefully.
   - Use the specific tribe, nation, or community name when known and relevant.
   - Do not collapse distinct tribal nations into generic terms when specificity matters.

6. Avoid deficit framing.
   - Do not describe communities only through disparities, risk, noncompliance, poverty, crime, or need.
   - Balance data with context, services, assets, and structural factors where relevant.

## Preferred rewrites

- `people without current immigration status` instead of `illegals`.
- `people who prefer Spanish` instead of `non-English people`.
- `Black residents` or another appropriate self-identified term instead of vague or outdated labels.
- `immigrant communities` instead of `foreign communities`.
- `tribal nations` or the specific nation name instead of generic references when specificity is needed.

## Escalate when
- The content concerns civil rights, discrimination, immigration enforcement, law enforcement data, demographic reporting, tribal consultation, federal program requirements, or community conflict.
- The content will be translated, used for language access notices, or distributed to a specific cultural community.
