# Subskill: Ownership and Review Cycle

## Purpose
Assign clear content stewardship and a review cadence so public content remains accurate, accountable, and maintainable.

## Use when
Use this subskill when creating, migrating, auditing, revising, retiring, or assigning responsibility for content.

## Ownership fields
Every governed content item should have:

- Primary content owner.
- Backup owner.
- Publishing contact.
- Subject matter expert.
- Approver.
- Source of truth.
- Last reviewed date.
- Next review due date.
- Review trigger conditions.

## Review cadence guidance

- **Monthly:** emergency, high-change service availability, deadlines, active campaigns, grants, procurement, temporary programs.
- **Quarterly:** forms, fees, eligibility, high-traffic services, cross-department content, operational instructions.
- **Semiannual:** program pages, landing pages, policy explainers, resident service guides.
- **Annual:** stable evergreen content, department descriptions, general contact pages.
- **Event-triggered:** ordinance changes, fee changes, form updates, vendor changes, staffing/contact changes, system changes, emergency status changes, Board action, law/policy changes.

## Stewardship checks
Ask:

1. Who is accountable for factual accuracy?
2. Who can approve changes?
3. What source proves the content is current?
4. How often does the information change?
5. What resident harm occurs if the content is wrong?
6. What event should force immediate review?

## Guardrail
Do not mark content as publication-ready if no owner or source of truth is known, except for draft planning work.
