# Subskill: Legal and Policy Risk

## Purpose
Identify content that may require legal, policy, executive, or program-authority review before publication or revision.

## Use when
Use this subskill for content involving rights, obligations, eligibility, enforcement, appeals, hearings, fees, deadlines, penalties, permits, licenses, ordinances, regulations, public notices, official policies, or legal disclaimers.

## High-risk indicators
Escalate when content:

- States who is eligible or ineligible for a service, benefit, permit, exemption, or appeal.
- Imposes deadlines, fees, penalties, conditions, documentation requirements, or compliance duties.
- Describes enforcement, citations, inspections, denials, revocations, or appeals.
- Interprets law, ordinance, regulation, code, policy, or administrative procedure.
- Makes promises about outcomes, timelines, legal rights, confidentiality, or guarantees.
- Contains disclaimers, liability language, or terms of use.
- Discusses protected classes, civil rights, due process, accessibility, language access, or discrimination risk.
- Changes a published policy, rule, or procedure.

## Safer handling

- Prefer quoting or linking to approved source material rather than paraphrasing legal requirements without review.
- Use plain language to explain process steps, but preserve legal terms where they affect rights or obligations.
- Separate general guidance from binding requirements.
- Avoid promises such as `will be approved`, `guaranteed`, or `always` unless sourced and approved.
- Use `may`, `must`, and `should` carefully. `Must` indicates a requirement.

## Required output

```markdown
### Legal and policy risk
- Legal/policy review required: yes/no/unknown
- Risk level:
- Triggering content:
- Safer revision approach:
- Approved source needed:
- Escalation path:
```

## Guardrail
Do not provide legal advice, determine legal sufficiency, or rewrite legal requirements in a way that changes their meaning.
