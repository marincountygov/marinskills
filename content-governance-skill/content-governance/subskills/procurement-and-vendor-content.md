# Subskill: Procurement and Vendor Content

## Purpose
Identify governance issues in procurement, contracting, solicitation, vendor, and purchasing-related content.

## Use when
Use this subskill for RFPs, RFQs, bids, solicitations, addenda, award notices, vendor pages, purchasing instructions, contract requirements, vendor references, product/service claims, sponsored content, or third-party platform language.

## Procurement flags
Escalate when content:

- Announces or changes a solicitation, bid, RFP, RFQ, addendum, deadline, scope, qualification, evaluation criterion, or submission instruction.
- Refers to a vendor as preferred, approved, endorsed, exclusive, best, or recommended.
- Describes contract terms, insurance, indemnity, payment, deliverables, or compliance requirements.
- Includes vendor logos, testimonials, product claims, implementation status, or commercial links.
- Posts award decisions, intent to award, protest instructions, or procurement timelines.
- Changes instructions during an active procurement.

## Vendor-neutral language
Use neutral language unless a contract or approved source supports a stronger claim:

- Prefer: `The County uses [system/vendor] for [purpose].`
- Avoid: `The County recommends [vendor]` unless explicitly approved.
- Prefer: `Submit by [deadline] using [method].`
- Avoid unsourced changes to deadlines, requirements, or evaluation criteria.

## Required output

```markdown
### Procurement and vendor review
- Procurement review required: yes/no/unknown
- Procurement status: planning / active / closed / award / contract administration / unknown
- Triggering content:
- Vendor-neutrality concerns:
- Approved source needed:
- Escalation path:
```

## Guardrails

- Do not modify active procurement instructions without procurement approval.
- Do not imply endorsement, preference, or exclusivity without an approved source.
- Do not summarize contract obligations in a way that changes requirements.
