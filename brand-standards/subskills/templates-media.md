# Templates and Media Sub-skill

Use this sub-skill for County of Marin presentations, reports, flyers, one-pagers, email signatures, social media graphics, video graphics, diagrams, and reusable templates.

This sub-skill covers visual design standards only. It intentionally omits accessibility requirements, which are handled by separate skills.

## General template principles

- Start from an official County or department template when one is supplied.
- Preserve official logo placement and proportions.
- Do not use the County logo as a watermark, background texture, decorative pattern, or repeated motif unless the official template already does so.
- Keep layouts simple, aligned, and structured.
- Use County colors and type guidance consistently, including the permitted foreground/background pairings and font-color rules in `subskills/color-typography.md`.
- Do not invent campaign marks, lockups, seals, taglines, or department logos.

## Email signatures

For County email signatures:

- Follow the Identity Style Guide email signature treatment when available.
- Keep name, title, department, phone, email, and web information clean and scannable.
- Avoid inspirational quotes, decorative images, excess icons, and unrelated badges.
- Use official social or County links only when appropriate.
- Do not attach large image-only signature blocks as a default design choice.

## Presentations

For slides:

- Use an official County or department deck when available.
- Place County identity consistently, usually in a footer, title slide, or end slide as the template directs.
- Use a single visual hierarchy for titles, section dividers, body copy, captions, and source notes.
- Keep slides sparse and structured.
- Use County palette colors for sectioning and emphasis.
- Avoid decorative transitions, heavy shadows, clip art, and unnecessary gradients.

## Reports, one-pagers, and flyers

- Use a clear title, date, department or program owner, and contact or next-step information.
- Use consistent heading levels and spacing.
- Prefer tables, callouts, and sidebars only when they improve scanning.
- Keep County identity visible but not oversized.
- Use official photos or approved imagery when possible.
- Do not crowd pages with competing logos.

## Social media graphics

- Use approved County identity treatment for avatars or profile graphics.
- Keep text short and visually clear.
- Use official department or campaign marks only when supplied.
- Avoid recreating the logo inside the post artwork when the platform avatar already carries identity, unless a template calls for it.
- Use visual hierarchy suitable for mobile viewing.

## Video graphics

- Use official logo artwork on opening cards, lower thirds, or end cards as the guide or template directs.
- Preserve clear space and minimum readable size.
- Keep title cards simple.
- Do not animate the official logo in a way that distorts, breaks apart, recolors, or trivializes it.
- Keep lower thirds consistent across a series.

## Forms and reusable layouts

- Use consistent field labels, section headings, spacing, and instructions.
- Keep the County or department identity visible without over-branding routine administrative forms.
- Do not create competing visual systems inside a single form.
- Keep visual states, labels, and helper text consistent across a set.

## Review checklist

- Is official identity used correctly?
- Is the medium-specific template followed?
- Is the layout aligned and uncluttered?
- Are color and typography consistent with County guidance?
- Are photos, icons, and graphics appropriate for a public County communication?
- Are approvals or official source assets needed?

## Response pattern

When producing or reviewing templates/media, return:

- Recommended layout approach.
- Identity placement.
- Color/type treatment.
- Medium-specific notes.
- Missing official assets.
- Approval or handoff notes.
