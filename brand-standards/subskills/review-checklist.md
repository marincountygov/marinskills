# County of Marin Design Review Checklist

Use this checklist to review draft designs, mockups, documents, slides, websites, emails, social media assets, and video graphics against County of Marin visual identity and design expectations.

This checklist excludes accessibility requirements because those are handled by separate accessibility skills.

## 1. Identity and logo

- [ ] Official County logo artwork is used when a logo is needed.
- [ ] Logo is not recreated, redrawn, distorted, cropped, recolored, outlined, shadowed, or decorated.
- [ ] Clear space is preserved.
- [ ] Minimum size guidance is respected.
- [ ] Logo variant suits the background and medium.
- [ ] Department identity does not compete with or obscure County identity.
- [ ] Co-branding is balanced and does not imply unauthorized endorsement.
- [ ] Website treatment follows County/department website logo guidance.
- [ ] Department websites include County Primary Logo in the footer linking to the County homepage when applicable.

## 2. Color

- [ ] Colors are from the County palette or an approved project/campaign palette.
- [ ] Accent colors are used sparingly and consistently.
- [ ] Color supports hierarchy and meaning.
- [ ] Backgrounds do not visually fight with the logo or text.
- [ ] Charts use a restrained number of colors.
- [ ] Decorative gradients, textures, or shadows are avoided unless part of an approved template.

## 3. Typography

- [ ] Official logo typography is not altered.
- [ ] Body copy uses County-approved or template-specified fonts.
- [ ] Heading levels are consistent.
- [ ] Type sizes and weights create clear hierarchy.
- [ ] All caps, bold, underlining, and decorative styling are restrained.
- [ ] Captions, labels, source notes, and dates are consistent.

## 4. Layout and composition

- [ ] Layout uses a clear grid or alignment system.
- [ ] Margins and spacing are consistent.
- [ ] Visual density is appropriate for the medium.
- [ ] Primary message is clear at a glance.
- [ ] Images support the message and do not appear generic or decorative without purpose.
- [ ] Icons are stylistically consistent.
- [ ] Calls to action or next steps are visually clear.

## 5. Medium-specific checks

### Email signature

- [ ] Signature is concise and follows County identity guidance.
- [ ] No unnecessary quotes, badges, decorative images, or oversized graphics.

### Presentation

- [ ] Title slide, section slides, and end slide use consistent County identity.
- [ ] Slides are not overcrowded.
- [ ] Charts and diagrams use clear labeling and County-appropriate styling.

### Social media

- [ ] Avatar/profile identity uses approved treatment.
- [ ] Post graphic is legible on mobile.
- [ ] Logo is not overused when the profile avatar already establishes identity.

### Video

- [ ] Opening card, lower thirds, and end card follow a consistent system.
- [ ] Logo is not distorted by animation or motion treatment.
- [ ] Title and name treatments are consistent.

### Reports/flyers/one-pagers

- [ ] Title, date, department/program owner, and contact/next-step information are clear.
- [ ] Identity is present but not oversized.
- [ ] Tables, callouts, and sidebars improve scanning rather than cluttering the page.

## 6. Approval and source files

- [ ] Official logo files are available.
- [ ] Official template is available when one exists.
- [ ] Public Information Office or department review is requested where needed.
- [ ] Legal, privacy, records, procurement, or public notice review is flagged where relevant.
- [ ] Accessibility review is routed to the separate accessibility skills where relevant.

Use this format:

**Required fixes:**

- ...

**Out of scope for this skill:**
