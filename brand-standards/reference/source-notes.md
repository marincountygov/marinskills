# Source Notes

This package is based on publicly available County of Marin design identity material available at the time the package was drafted.

## County of Marin Identity Style Guide

Primary source:

- County of Marin Identity Style Guide, prepared by L Studio, updated May 2014, as published through marincounty.gov.
- The public County page describes the guide as a resource for consistent and effective use of the County logo across communications and materials.

Guidance incorporated from the public identity guide includes:

- Official County logo use.
- Primary, secondary, grayscale, black, and white logo treatments.
- Logo clear space.
- Logo minimum size.
- Do-not-recreate and do-not-distort rules.
- County color palette.
- Typography guidance, including Futura for official logo/logotype artwork, Arial Regular for print body copy, Georgia as a serif option, and Verdana for website typography.
- Department website footer logo placement.
- Social media avatar treatment.
- Video identity treatment.
- Email signature treatment.

## Deliberate exclusions

This package intentionally excludes:

- County digital accessibility requirements.
- WCAG requirements.
- PDF remediation guidance.
- Screen reader, keyboard, alt text, caption, reading-order, heading-structure, and focus-state review.

Those topics should be handled by separate accessibility skills.

## Known limitations

- Official logo artwork is not included in this package.
- Internal MINE intranet templates are not included.
- Department-specific brand extensions may exist and should be supplied by the user or confirmed with the responsible County team.
- The public identity guide is older than the current marincounty.gov visual interface; this skill preserves explicit published standards and treats modern implementation details as project-specific unless publicly documented.
