# Spanish Content Review Subskill

## Purpose
Review Spanish-language content for clarity, consistency, tone, and alignment with the English source.

## Use when
Use this subskill when asked to review Spanish content, compare Spanish and English versions, or improve Spanish-language public content.

## Review dimensions

### Meaning alignment
Check whether the Spanish version preserves:

- Resident task and next steps
- Eligibility criteria
- Deadlines and dates
- Required documents
- Fees and payment instructions
- Contact information
- Legal, health, safety, emergency, or benefits-related meaning

### Plain-language Spanish
Prefer:

- Direct sentence structure
- Familiar public-service wording
- Consistent verbs for actions residents must take
- Clear explanations of official terms
- Concrete instructions over abstract descriptions

Avoid:

- Literal calques that sound unnatural
- Overly formal bureaucratic phrasing when a clearer alternative is available
- Regionally obscure terms when a broadly understood term is needed
- Inconsistent treatment of usted/tú; default to formal `usted` for public agency content unless a style guide says otherwise

### Terminology
Flag inconsistencies in:

- Program names
- Department names
- Form names
- Benefit names
- Legal terms
- Public health and public safety terms
- Digital service labels such as apply, renew, report, appeal, request, register, and pay

## Risk handling
High-risk Spanish content should be reviewed by a qualified translator, bilingual subject-matter reviewer, or authorized language-access reviewer before publication. High-risk content includes rights, benefits, eligibility, legal obligations, enforcement, medical guidance, emergency instructions, deadlines, appeals, and safety information.

## Output pattern

Provide:

1. **Overall assessment** — clear, mostly clear with edits, or needs qualified review.
2. **Source-alignment issues** — what meaning changed, narrowed, expanded, or became ambiguous.
3. **Terminology notes** — inconsistent or risky terms.
4. **Suggested Spanish revisions** — concise edits with rationale.
5. **Escalation notes** — when human review is needed before publication.
