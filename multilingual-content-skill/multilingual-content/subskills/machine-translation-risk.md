# Machine Translation Risk Subskill

## Purpose
Assess when machine translation may be acceptable, when it requires human review, and when it is inappropriate for publication without qualified review.

## Use when
Use this subskill when asked whether automated translation can be used, reviewed, published, or relied on for resident-facing content.

## Risk levels

### Lower risk
Machine translation may be usable with light human review for:

- General announcements
- Basic navigation labels
- Non-binding event descriptions
- Informal internal drafts
- Early translation discovery work

### Medium risk
Machine translation may assist drafting but should receive human review for:

- Service instructions
- Applications and registrations
- Payment instructions
- Appointment scheduling
- Required documents
- Program descriptions
- Public notices with operational consequences

### High risk
Machine translation should not be published without qualified review for:

- Legal rights or obligations
- Eligibility for benefits or services
- Appeals, hearings, enforcement, violations, or penalties
- Medical, behavioral health, public health, or safety guidance
- Emergency instructions
- Evacuation, shelter, or disaster response information
- Privacy, consent, confidentiality, or data-use notices
- Deadlines that affect rights, benefits, payments, or compliance

## Review guidance

For medium- and high-risk content, recommend:

- Qualified translator review
- Bilingual subject-matter review
- Program owner review
- Legal, privacy, health, public safety, or emergency communications review when applicable

## Output pattern

Provide:

1. **Machine-translation risk level** — lower, medium, or high.
2. **Reason** — specific content features that create risk.
3. **Allowed use** — draft only, publish after bilingual review, or do not publish without qualified review.
4. **Required review** — who should review before publication.
5. **Risky segments** — exact lines or sections that need attention.
