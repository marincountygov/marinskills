# Language Access Notices Subskill

## Purpose
Create or improve notices that tell residents how to get language help, interpretation, translation, or assistance in another language.

## Use when
Use this subskill for service pages, forms, public notices, emergency notices, meetings, hearings, benefits pages, enforcement-related notices, and resident-facing transactions.

## Notice quality criteria

A good language-access notice states:

- Language help is available.
- What type of help is available, such as interpretation, translation, or help understanding a notice.
- How to request help.
- Whether there is a deadline or lead time.
- Whether help is free, if applicable and authorized.
- A phone number, email, form, office, or other contact method.
- Any emergency or time-sensitive instructions.

## Placement guidance

Place notices where residents need them:

- Near the top of high-risk notices
- Near contact information
- On forms and application instructions
- On meeting, hearing, or public comment pages
- On emergency or public health pages
- Near downloadable documents that are only available in one language

## Drafting guidance

- Use direct language.
- Avoid legalistic phrasing unless required.
- Do not bury the notice in a footer when language access is central to completing the task.
- Keep the notice short enough to translate accurately.
- Match the notice to the service context.

## Output pattern

Provide:

1. **Notice assessment** — sufficient, needs revision, missing critical information, or high-risk routing needed.
2. **Recommended placement** — where the notice should appear.
3. **Draft or revised notice** — concise and task-specific.
4. **Missing information** — contact method, lead time, cost statement, or responsible office.
5. **Review routing** — language-access, program, legal, emergency communications, or meeting clerk review as applicable.
