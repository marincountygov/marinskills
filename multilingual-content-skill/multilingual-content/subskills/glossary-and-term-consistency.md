# Glossary and Term Consistency Subskill

## Purpose
Control recurring terms so multilingual content uses consistent, accurate, and resident-centered language.

## Use when
Use this subskill when content contains program names, service names, form names, legal terms, benefits, eligibility categories, technical terms, or repeated task labels.

## Glossary candidates
Flag terms that are:

- Official names: departments, offices, programs, services, boards, commissions
- Transaction labels: apply, renew, report, register, request, pay, appeal, schedule, check eligibility
- Legal or policy terms: ordinance, hearing, notice, appeal, enforcement, violation, exemption
- Benefits and eligibility terms: household, income, resident, applicant, dependent, qualifying, documentation
- Health and safety terms: evacuation, shelter, exposure, isolation, outbreak, emergency alert
- Forms and documents: application, affidavit, permit, license, receipt, proof, notice
- Digital labels: account, confirmation, submission, upload, status, appointment

## Glossary table format
Use this format when a glossary is useful:

| Source term | Approved translation or treatment | Context | Notes | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Term | Translation, retain in English, or translate plus English in parentheses | Where it appears | Risk, nuance, or usage rule | Program or reviewer |

## Consistency rules

- Do not translate official names inconsistently across pages.
- Decide whether to translate, retain, or pair official names before publication.
- Use one approved rendering for each resident task unless context requires otherwise.
- Preserve form numbers and official titles.
- Keep glossary notes close to source content when handing off to translators.

## Output pattern

Provide:

1. **Glossary candidates** — table or list.
2. **Consistency problems** — same term used differently or different terms used for same concept.
3. **Recommended controlled terms** — proposed wording and treatment.
4. **Open questions** — terms requiring program owner or language-access decision.
