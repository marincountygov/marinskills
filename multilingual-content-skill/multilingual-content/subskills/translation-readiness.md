# Translation Readiness Subskill

## Purpose
Prepare source content so it can be translated accurately, consistently, and efficiently.

## Use when
Use this subskill when content will be translated or localized, especially if the source text contains complex policy language, program instructions, deadlines, forms, eligibility criteria, or resident actions.

## What to improve

### Structure
- Put the resident task near the top.
- Use headings that describe actions or topics.
- Break procedures into ordered steps.
- Separate eligibility, required documents, fees, deadlines, and contact information.
- Avoid dense paragraphs.

### Sentence quality
- Prefer short sentences.
- Use active voice where possible.
- Keep one condition or requirement per sentence.
- Avoid nested clauses and stacked modifiers.
- Avoid idioms, humor, metaphors, and culture-specific references.

### Terminology
- Use the same term for the same concept every time.
- Avoid switching between synonyms such as application, request, submission, and filing unless each means something different.
- Spell out acronyms on first use.
- Preserve official program, department, ordinance, and form names.

### Risk-sensitive details
Check that these are explicit and unambiguous:

- Who is eligible
- What documents are required
- How to apply or request service
- Fees and payment methods
- Deadlines and effective dates
- Appeal or complaint options
- Contact channels
- Privacy or confidentiality statements
- Emergency or safety instructions

## Output pattern

Provide:

1. **Translation-readiness assessment** — ready, needs revision, or high-risk review needed.
2. **Key risks** — terms, ambiguity, legal/policy dependencies, missing context.
3. **Recommended source edits** — concise revision or annotated change list.
4. **Glossary candidates** — terms that should be controlled before translation.
5. **Review routing** — translator, bilingual reviewer, program owner, legal/policy reviewer, or emergency communications lead as applicable.
