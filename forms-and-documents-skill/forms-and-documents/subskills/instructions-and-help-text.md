# Subskill: Instructions and Help Text

## Use this when
Writing or reviewing instructions, helper text, tooltips, examples, error-prevention language, upload guidance, and completion steps.

## Goals

- Help residents complete the form or document correctly the first time.
- Put instructions near the action they support.
- Reduce long front-loaded instruction blocks.
- Explain consequences, prerequisites, and next steps clearly.

## Guidance

### Put help at the point of need
Place guidance next to the field, section, or step it supports.

Avoid making residents remember instructions from the top of a page or separate packet.

### Lead with the action
Use imperative, task-focused language:

- Upload a copy of your lease.
- Enter the address where the issue is happening.
- Choose all services you are requesting.
- Sign and date the form before you submit it.

### Explain why only when it helps completion
Add the reason when it affects trust, accuracy, or willingness to provide information:

- We use this address to confirm service area eligibility.
- We use your email only to send status updates about this request.

### Define acceptable documents and formats
For uploads or attachments, state:

- What document is acceptable
- Whether copies or photos are allowed
- File type and size limits if known
- Whether documents must be current, signed, or dated

Example pattern:

> Upload one document that shows your current address, such as a utility bill, lease, or government letter dated within the last 60 days.

### Use examples for unfamiliar concepts
Examples should clarify, not expand policy.

Good examples:

- Examples of income: wages, benefits, pension, child support, or self-employment income.
- Examples of proof of identity: driver's license, state ID, passport, or other government-issued photo ID.

### Avoid vague help text
Replace:

- Complete all applicable sections.
- Provide supporting documentation.
- Ensure information is accurate.

With:

- Complete sections 1 through 4. Complete section 5 only if you are requesting a fee waiver.
- Attach a copy of your signed lease or most recent rent receipt.
- Review your phone number and mailing address before you submit.

## Review checklist

- Instructions are close to the relevant task.
- The first words tell residents what to do.
- Help text prevents likely mistakes.
- Required documents are named clearly.
- Time limits, dates, formats, and consequences are explicit.
- Instructions do not repeat or conflict with other sections.
