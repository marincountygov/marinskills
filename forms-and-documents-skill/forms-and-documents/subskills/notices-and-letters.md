# Subskill: Notices and Letters

## Use this when
Creating or reviewing notices, letters, determinations, approvals, denials, reminders, warnings, requests for information, and follow-up correspondence.

## Goals

- Make the purpose of the notice immediately clear.
- Tell the resident what changed, what they need to do, by when, and what happens next.
- Preserve required legal or program language while improving structure and plain-language framing.

## Standard structure

1. Clear subject or heading
2. One-sentence reason for the notice
3. Decision, status, or requested action
4. Deadline or effective date
5. What the resident must do next
6. Documents, payment, appointment, or evidence needed
7. Consequences of not responding, if applicable
8. Contact and help options
9. Rights, appeal, or review information when required

## Writing guidance

### Put the main message first
Start with the point of the letter.

Examples:

- Your application was approved.
- We need more information before we can process your request.
- Your hearing is scheduled for June 12, 2026.
- You must renew your permit by July 1, 2026.

### Separate required action from background
Do not bury resident tasks inside policy explanations.

Use headings such as:

- What you need to do
- Deadline
- Documents to send
- How to appeal
- How to get help

### Preserve but isolate mandatory language
If legal language is required, keep it accurate but place it under a clear heading. Add a plain-language summary only if it does not alter meaning.

Pattern:

- Plain-language summary
- Required notice language
- Contact for questions

### Be precise about dates and consequences
Avoid relative deadlines when possible.

Better:

- We must receive your documents by July 15, 2026.

Weaker:

- Respond within 10 days.

If relative deadlines are legally required, include both the rule and a calculated date when available.

### Use neutral, respectful wording
Avoid blame-oriented phrasing.

Replace:

- You failed to provide proof.

With:

- We have not received proof of income.

## Review checklist

- The first paragraph states the reason for the notice.
- Required actions are separated from background.
- Deadlines and effective dates are clear.
- Consequences are stated accurately and calmly.
- Contact options are easy to find.
- Legal, appeal, or rights language is flagged for appropriate review.
