# Subskill: PDF vs HTML Decision

## Use this when
Deciding whether content should be published as a web page, online form, PDF, downloadable document, printed packet, or hybrid.

## Core rule
Use HTML for content residents need to read, search, translate, complete on mobile, revisit, or act on. Use PDF or downloadable files only when the format serves a specific operational, legal, archival, signature, or print need.

## Prefer HTML when

- The content explains a service, process, deadline, fee, or requirement.
- Residents need to find the information through search.
- The content changes often.
- The content is long but can be structured into headings and sections.
- The content is primarily instructions.
- The content is a checklist, eligibility guide, FAQ, or how-to page.
- The content should work well on mobile.
- The content needs translation or multilingual publishing.

## Prefer an online form when

- Residents must submit information to start or complete a transaction.
- The agency needs structured data.
- Validation can prevent errors.
- Conditional logic can reduce irrelevant questions.
- Status confirmation and next steps matter.

## Keep or provide PDF when

- A wet signature, official form layout, or print workflow is required.
- The document must be submitted to another agency in a fixed format.
- The document is an official record, signed notice, agenda packet, map, or complex formatted document.
- Residents need an offline copy.
- The document is designed for printing and physical distribution.

## Use both HTML and PDF when

- HTML can explain the task and PDF is required for submission.
- A public notice requires a downloadable official version but residents also need a readable summary.
- A packet is too complex to remove immediately, but a web page can guide residents to the right document.

## Decision output pattern

When asked to assess a document, provide:

- Recommended format: HTML, online form, PDF, hybrid, or retire
- Reasoning
- What content should move to HTML
- What content should remain downloadable
- Risks or dependencies
- Implementation checklist

## Review checklist

- The recommended format supports the resident task.
- The decision does not preserve PDFs by default.
- Official or signature requirements are identified.
- Frequently changing content is not trapped in static files.
- Download pages explain what the file is, who needs it, and what to do with it.
