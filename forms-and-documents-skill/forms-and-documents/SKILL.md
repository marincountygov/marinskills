# Forms and Documents Skill

## Purpose
Improve forms, notices, letters, packets, downloadable documents, and decisions about when content should be HTML instead of PDF.

Use this skill when asked to create, review, rewrite, convert, or assess:

- Online forms
- Paper forms
- Notices and letters
- Application packets
- Download pages
- PDFs, Word documents, and other downloadable files
- Instructions, help text, and field labels
- Decisions about whether content belongs in HTML, PDF, both, or another format

## Primary outcome
Help residents complete required tasks with fewer errors, fewer support contacts, fewer abandoned transactions, and clearer understanding of what will happen next.

## Operating principles

1. Start with the resident task.
   - Identify what the resident is trying to do.
   - Identify what the agency needs to collect, explain, or confirm.
   - Remove anything that does not support the task, legal requirement, or operational need.

2. Prefer task completion over document preservation.
   - Do not assume an existing PDF or packet should remain as-is.
   - Recommend HTML when residents need to read, search, translate, complete on mobile, or revisit information frequently.
   - Keep downloadable documents when signatures, attachments, official forms, offline use, or print workflows are necessary.

3. Reduce interpretation burden.
   - Use direct labels, clear instructions, examples, and conditional guidance.
   - Put help where users need it, not in a detached instruction block.
   - Replace agency-centered phrasing with resident-centered action language.

4. Design for error prevention.
   - Clarify eligibility, required documents, deadlines, fees, formats, and consequences before residents begin.
   - Identify common mistakes and add preventive guidance.
   - Use confirmation and next-step language after submission or download.

5. Separate editorial advice from official determinations.
   - Flag legal, policy, records-retention, privacy, accessibility, procurement, or program-rule questions for the appropriate skill or human reviewer.
   - Do not invent statutory requirements, retention rules, or eligibility criteria.

- `subskills/form-field-design.md`: Field labels, input formats, required/optional fields, grouping, validation, and form simplification.
- `subskills/instructions-and-help-text.md`: Inline instructions, examples, helper text, error prevention, and task guidance.
- `subskills/notices-and-letters.md`: Resident-facing letters, notices, determinations, reminders, warnings, approvals, denials, and follow-up messages.
- `subskills/pdf-vs-html-decision.md`: Decide whether content should be HTML, PDF, both, or another format.
- `subskills/document-conversion.md`: Convert packets, PDFs, Word documents, and long documents into digital-first pages or flows.
- `subskills/download-page-patterns.md`: Improve pages that host forms, packets, PDFs, templates, brochures, agendas, or downloadable resources.

## Standard workflow

1. Identify the document or form type.
2. Identify the resident task, agency task, and required outcome.
3. Identify whether the content is informational, transactional, evidentiary, legal, or operational.
4. Decide whether the current format supports the task.
5. Review for missing prerequisites, confusing sequence, duplicate questions, unclear ownership, and weak next steps.
6. Rewrite or restructure using the relevant subskill.
7. Provide a short implementation checklist.

## Required outputs when reviewing content

When useful, provide:

- A diagnosis of the current issue
- A recommended format or structure
- Revised labels, instructions, headings, or page copy
- Risks or escalation items
- A checklist for publishing or implementation

## Quality checklist

Before finalizing, verify that the result answers:

- Who is this for?
- What can the resident do here?
- What information or documents do they need before starting?
- How long will it take?
- What happens after submission, mailing, upload, download, or review?
- What are the deadlines, fees, consequences, and contact options?
- Is the format appropriate: HTML, form, PDF, letter, packet, or hybrid?
- Does the content avoid unnecessary legalese, duplication, and agency jargon?

## Escalation triggers

Escalate or flag for review when content involves:

- Legal rights, appeal rights, penalties, enforcement, denial, or termination
- Personally identifiable information or sensitive case details
- Public records retention, official records, or evidence collection
- Program eligibility rules, statutory requirements, or regulated notices
- Procurement, contracts, vendor forms, or insurance requirements
- Emergency orders, public notices, or time-sensitive safety information
- Accessibility remediation or conformance requirements handled by a separate skill
