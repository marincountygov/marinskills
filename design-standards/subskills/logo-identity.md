# Logo and Identity Sub-skill

Use this sub-skill for County of Marin logo treatment, lockups, placement, scale, co-branding, department marks, social media avatars, and video identity.

## Logo hierarchy

1. Primary Logo: Civic Center symbol plus County of Marin logotype. Use this as the first-choice representation of the County.
2. Secondary Logo: reproportioned version for smaller or constrained spaces where County of Marin must remain legible.
3. Logotypes: typographic representations for situations where size constraints or logo clutter call for simpler identification.
4. Symbol alone: use only in select approved applications.

## Non-negotiable logo rules

- Use provided official artwork.
- Never recreate the logo.
- Never alter, stretch, distort, rotate, recolor, crop, outline, add shadows, or otherwise modify the artwork.
- Maintain breathing room around the logo.
- Never overlap the logo with other logos, graphics, photos, copy, or decorative elements.
- Use the black-line version in most ordinary applications.
- Use reversed white logo artwork only on dark or black backgrounds.
- Use approved color variants only for special applications.

## Minimum size

- Primary Logo: minimum width is 1.25 inches, measured from the left edge of the dome to the dotted end of the horizon line.
- Secondary Logo: minimum width is 0.75 inches, measured from the left edge of the dome to the right margin of the logotype.
- Digital equivalents must preserve legibility. If the logo becomes unclear, use the secondary logo or logotype rather than scaling below readable size.

## Color logo use

Permitted color treatments include the approved circular seal, square patch, gold symbol, and all-gold version. The gold specification must not be altered.

Gold:

- PMS 7405 uncoated
- PMS 7406 coated
- CMYK: 11, 28, 90, 0
- RGB: 229, 181, 59
- HEX: `#e5b53b`

Metallic gold is for print only and should use PMS 8643 or a matching matte metallic foil.

## Websites

- The County homepage uses the Secondary Logo in the upper-left area.
- Department websites with their own branding must use the County Primary Logo in the standard page footer to link back to the County homepage.
- For department websites, place the Primary Logo in the bottom-right corner, in black or white, preferably with a dotted line to its left.

## Social media avatars

Use the Secondary Logo or Secondary Logotype to preserve legibility of “County of Marin” at small sizes. Do not use complex full layouts as avatars.

## Video identity

- Place County or department logo identity at the beginning and end of videos.
- Use the Primary Logo when no department logo exists.
- Third-party vendor logos may appear at the end with the County Primary Logo.
- Keep logo size legible and proportional. Do not allow vendor marks to dominate County identity when the video represents County work.

## Co-branding and departments

When a department has its own recognized logo or graphic style, allow appropriate department expression while preserving County family identity. For unclear co-branding, request or check official department guidance.

## Review questions

- Is the official logo file being used?
- Is the chosen logo version appropriate for the space?
- Is the logo legible at final size?
- Is there adequate clear space?
- Has the logo been altered in any way?
- Does the footer or end card include required County identity where applicable?
