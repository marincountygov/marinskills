# Subskill: Content Lifecycle

## Purpose

Use this subskill to define how content moves from idea to publication, maintenance, review, archival, and retirement.

## Core rule

Content is not complete at publication. Every content item needs an owner, review cadence, source of truth, and end-of-life plan.

## Lifecycle states

### Proposed

The content has been requested but not yet validated.

Required checks:

- User need.
- Audience.
- Purpose.
- Owner.
- Existing content overlap.
- Source of truth.

### Planned

The content has a validated purpose and is scheduled for creation or revision.

Required checks:

- Content type.
- Location.
- Metadata.
- Draft owner.
- Reviewers.
- Target publish date.

### Draft

The content is being written or assembled.

Required checks:

- User-centered structure.
- Required facts.
- Related links.
- Metadata.
- Review path.

### In review

The content is awaiting subject-matter, editorial, legal, policy, communications, or other review.

Required checks:

- Reviewer names or roles.
- Review due date.
- Approval criteria.
- Required changes.

### Published

The content is live and available to users.

Required checks:

- Owner.
- Publish date.
- Last reviewed date.
- Next review date.
- Monitoring metric.

### Needs update

The content is live but inaccurate, incomplete, stale, duplicative, or misaligned.

Required checks:

- Risk level.
- Required correction.
- Owner.
- Deadline.

### Archived

The content is retained for reference but no longer active.

Required checks:

- Archive reason.
- Archive date.
- Replacement or redirect.
- Retention or records considerations if applicable.

### Retired or deleted

The content is removed because it is no longer useful, accurate, required, or maintainable.

Required checks:

- Deletion rationale.
- Redirect plan if there is traffic or links.
- Confirmation that no active obligation requires the content.

## Review cadence guidance

Use the shortest cadence needed to keep the content trustworthy.

| Content type | Typical review cadence |
|---|---|
| Service page | Every 6 to 12 months, or when service rules change |
| Policy or requirement | At effective-date changes or at least annually |
| High-risk public information | Every 3 to 6 months |
| Campaign page | Before launch, during campaign, and at campaign end |
| News item | Review for archival after the relevance window closes |
| Event | Before event and immediately after event |
| Directory or index | Every 3 to 6 months |
| Downloadable form | At each form, policy, or process change |

## Ownership requirements

Each content item should have:

- Business owner.
- Editorial owner.
- Subject-matter reviewer.
- Source of truth.
- Backup contact where possible.
- Escalation path for urgent corrections.

## Trigger-based reviews

Review content immediately when:

- A law, policy, fee, deadline, eligibility rule, contact, form, location, or process changes.
- A program launches, ends, changes name, or changes ownership.
- Analytics show failed searches, abandonment, low conversion, or high support demand.
- Users report confusion or errors.
- Related content is moved or retired.
- A campaign, emergency, event, or seasonal window ends.

## Decision rules

- Do not recommend publishing ownerless content.
- Do not treat news as the long-term source of truth.
- Time-limited content must have an end date or review trigger.
- High-risk content needs a more frequent review cadence.
- Content with no owner, no use, no legal or operational need, and no current accuracy should be retired or archived.
