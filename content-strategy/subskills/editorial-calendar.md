# Subskill: Editorial Calendar

## Purpose

Use this subskill to plan, schedule, coordinate, and maintain content across channels and time periods.

## Core rule

An editorial calendar is not just a publishing schedule. It should connect audience need, channel, owner, review process, publication timing, lifecycle, and success measure.

## When to use

Use for:

- Campaigns.
- Seasonal content.
- News and announcements.
- Public meetings or events.
- Program launches.
- Recurring updates.
- Cross-channel communications.
- Content maintenance cycles.

## Calendar fields

A useful editorial calendar includes:

- Item title.
- Content type.
- Audience.
- User intent.
- Channel.
- Owner.
- Draft due date.
- Review due date.
- Approval date.
- Publish date.
- Refresh date.
- Unpublish or archive date.
- Dependencies.
- Status.
- Success measure.

## Status labels

Use consistent labels:

- Proposed.
- Planned.
- Drafting.
- In review.
- Approved.
- Scheduled.
- Published.
- Needs update.
- Archived.
- Retired.

## Channel planning

Identify whether the item belongs on:

- Website.
- Intranet.
- Email newsletter.
- Social media.
- Press release.
- Printed material.
- Partner toolkit.
- Public meeting agenda.
- Document library.
- Service portal.

Do not duplicate the same full content across channels unless there is a clear need. Use channels to route users to the source of truth.

## Timing questions

Ask:

- What user need or event drives the timing?
- What must be published before this item?
- What reviews or approvals are required?
- Is the content seasonal, recurring, urgent, or evergreen?
- When does the content become stale?
- What happens after the publish window ends?

## Campaign calendar pattern

For campaigns, plan in phases:

### Pre-launch

- Define audience and goals.
- Prepare source-of-truth page.
- Draft supporting content.
- Confirm metadata and tracking.
- Schedule review and approval.

### Launch

- Publish core content.
- Coordinate channel releases.
- Monitor user response.
- Correct issues quickly.

### Sustain

- Refresh messaging.
- Answer recurring questions.
- Adjust channels based on performance.

### Closeout

- Unpublish or archive campaign-specific material.
- Update evergreen content.
- Record performance and lessons learned.

## Decision rules

- Do not schedule publication without an owner.
- Do not schedule time-limited content without a refresh, archive, or unpublish date.
- For multi-channel content, identify the single source of truth.
- For recurring content, define the recurrence pattern and who confirms each cycle.
- For campaigns, plan closeout before launch.
