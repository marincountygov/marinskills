# Email Accessibility Skill

Use this skill to create and review accessible County of Marin email content, including newsletters, service notices, alerts, transactional messages, and internal communications.

## Purpose

Email is a major public communication channel. County emails should be accessible, readable, brand-consistent, and effective across email clients, devices, and assistive technologies.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill for:

- Newsletters.
- Public notices.
- Service updates.
- Emergency or urgent alerts.
- Transactional emails.
- Event invitations.
- Internal staff emails.
- Email templates.
- Automated email notifications.

## Accessibility requirements

Emails should:

- Use semantic headings where supported.
- Use a logical reading order.
- Use descriptive link text.
- Avoid “click here” and raw URLs when possible.
- Include alt text for informative images.
- Avoid image-only emails.
- Repeat essential image text in live text.
- Use sufficient color contrast.
- Avoid relying on color alone.
- Use readable font sizes.
- Keep layouts responsive and usable on mobile devices.
- Avoid overly complex tables or nested layouts where possible.
- Provide a plain-text or accessible fallback when appropriate.

## Plain-language requirements

Emails should:

- Use a clear subject line.
- Put the main message first.
- State who the message is for.
- State what action is needed, if any.
- Include deadlines and dates in complete form.
- Use short sections and clear headings.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon or acronyms.
- Include a clear contact path.

## Brand requirements

Emails should:

- Use approved County identity elements.
- Maintain consistent sender identity.
- Use a professional and service-oriented tone.
- Avoid visual clutter.
- Make calls to action clear and restrained.

## Review workflow

1. Review audience, sender, subject line, and main message.
2. Check structure, headings, links, and calls to action.
3. Review image use and alt text.
4. Check contrast, mobile readability, and layout.
5. Confirm plain-language and brand consistency.
6. Confirm that linked destination pages are accessible and current.

## Output format

When reviewing an email, provide:

- Accessibility status.
- Subject-line recommendation.
- Body-copy recommendations.
- Link and call-to-action issues.
- Image and alt-text issues.
- Mobile/layout concerns.
- Brand and plain-language notes.
