# Video and Audio Accessibility Skill

Use this skill to create, review, or remediate accessible County of Marin video and audio content.

## Purpose

Video and audio content must be accessible to people who are Deaf, hard of hearing, blind, have low vision, process information differently, or cannot use audio or video in their current environment.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill for:

- Public videos.
- Recorded meetings.
- Training videos.
- Social media videos.
- Livestreams.
- Podcasts.
- Audio announcements.
- Webinars.
- Public service announcements.

## Accessibility requirements

Video and audio content should include:

- Accurate captions for spoken dialogue and meaningful sounds.
- Transcripts for audio-only content and, when useful, video content.
- Audio description or an equivalent alternative for important visual-only information.
- Speaker identification when multiple speakers are present.
- Clear audio quality.
- Avoidance of flashing content that may trigger seizures.
- Accessible media player controls.
- Keyboard-operable playback controls.
- Sufficient color contrast for text overlays.
- Text overlays that remain on screen long enough to read.

## Caption requirements

Captions should:

- Match spoken content accurately.
- Identify speakers when needed.
- Include meaningful sound cues.
- Use proper punctuation and capitalization.
- Be synchronized with the audio.
- Avoid excessive line length.
- Remain readable against the background.

## Transcript requirements

Transcripts should:

- Include spoken content.
- Identify speakers.
- Include meaningful sound or visual context when needed.
- Be available near the media or linked clearly.
- Use accessible HTML or an accessible document format.

## Audio description guidance

Use audio description or an equivalent text alternative when important information is presented only visually, such as:

- On-screen instructions.
- Charts or maps.
- Demonstrations.
- Names, titles, or locations shown only as text.
- Critical actions without narration.

## Plain-language and brand requirements

Scripts and captions should:

- Use clear, direct language.
- Avoid unexplained acronyms.
- State the main message early.
- Include clear next steps.
- Match County tone and visual standards.

## Review workflow

1. Identify whether the media is audio-only, video-only, or audio-video.
2. Check captions, transcript, and audio description needs.
3. Review visual text, contrast, flashing content, and timing.
4. Confirm player accessibility.
5. Review plain-language and brand consistency.
6. Provide remediation steps before publication.

## Output format

When reviewing media, provide:

- Accessibility status.
- Caption issues.
- Transcript issues.
- Audio-description needs.
- Visual accessibility issues.
- Plain-language and brand notes.
- Publication readiness recommendation.
